Ingredients (Serves 2)
2 ribeye steaks (or sirloin steaks) about 3 cm thick.
1 cup Malbec wine, 2 tablespoons sugar (or honey), 1 beef bouillon cube, and a sprig of rosemary.
1 tablespoon cold butter.

Step-by-Step Preparation:
1 - Sear the meat: Heat a skillet with a drizzle of oil over high heat. Season the meat with salt and pepper and sear for 4-5 minutes per side (for medium-rare). Remove the meat and let it rest on a covered plate.
2 - Deglaze: In the same skillet (without washing it), pour in the Malbec wine. Scrape the bottom with a spatula to release the flavors of the meat.
3 - Reduce: Add the sugar, bouillon cube, and rosemary. Simmer over medium heat until the liquid has reduced by half and thickened (to a syrupy consistency).
4 - Emulsify: Turn off the heat, add the cold butter, and whisk vigorously to give the sauce a glossy, silky texture.
5 - Serve: Pour the hot reduction over the meat.
